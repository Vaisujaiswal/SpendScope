# Metrics

## North Star Metric

### High-Savings Qualified Leads Per Month

This is the most important metric because SpendScope is ultimately a lead-generation product for Credex, not a traditional daily-use SaaS tool.

The goal is not maximizing daily active users or session time. The real value comes from identifying companies with meaningful AI overspending and converting them into infrastructure optimization opportunities.

A “high-savings qualified lead” means:
- a completed audit
- estimated savings above a meaningful threshold (example: $500/month)
- valid lead capture information submitted

This metric directly connects product usage to potential business value.

---

# Input Metrics

## 1. Audit Completion Rate

Percentage of users who start the audit and successfully finish it.

This measures:
- onboarding friction
- form UX quality
- clarity of the value proposition

If users abandon the flow early, the product cannot generate qualified leads.

---

## 2. Lead Capture Conversion Rate

Percentage of completed audits that result in:
- email submission
- saved report
- consultation interest

This measures whether the audit results feel valuable enough for users to exchange contact information.

---

## 3. Shareable Audit URL Usage

Number of audits shared publicly through copied links or screenshots.

This matters because the product is designed with viral distribution in mind. Shared results can drive organic growth through founder communities, X, Reddit, and Slack groups.

---

# What I’d Instrument First

The first analytics events I would track are:

- homepage visits
- audit started
- audit completed
- report saved
- consultation CTA clicked
- shareable URL copied
- PDF export usage

These events would help identify:
- where users drop off
- which audit types convert best
- whether the viral sharing loop is actually working

---

# Pivot Decision Trigger

I would seriously reconsider the product direction if:

- audit completion rates stay below 25%
OR
- fewer than 5% of completed audits convert into saved reports or consultation interest

after multiple iterations.

That would suggest one of two problems:
- users do not trust the recommendations
- the perceived savings are not strong enough to justify follow-up action

The product only works if users believe the audit creates real operational or financial value.

---

# Why These Metrics Matter

SpendScope is a low-frequency B2B optimization product, not a consumer social app.

Most users will not use it daily. They may only revisit:
- quarterly
- during budgeting reviews
- while evaluating AI infrastructure changes

Because of that, metrics like:
- DAU
- time spent in app
- session frequency

are less meaningful than:
- audit quality
- lead quality
- conversion intent
- shareability
