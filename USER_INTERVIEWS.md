# USER INTERVIEWS

## Interview 1 — Computer Science Student 

**Stage:** Individual user building personal coding projects

The first interview was with a friend who regularly uses Claude and ChatGPT for coding help, debugging, and learning. They currently pay for subscriptions personally instead of through a company or team budget.

### Direct Quotes
- “I honestly don’t know how much I spend monthly on AI tools.”
- “I switched from Claude to ChatGPT because it felt more useful overall.”
- “AI tool costs are increasing really fast now.”

### Most Surprising Thing
The most surprising part was that they had switched tools based mainly on perceived usefulness, not actual pricing comparisons. They also admitted they rarely review subscriptions once activated.

### What It Changed About My Design
This conversation reinforced the importance of:
- simple benchmark messaging
- clearer savings visibility
- annual savings breakdowns

It also pushed me to make the audit results easier to understand visually instead of showing only technical pricing details.

## Interview 2 —  Student Freelancer

**Stage:** Individual freelancer using AI tools for productivity and writing tasks

The second interview was with a friend using ChatGPT regularly for freelancing, writing assistance, and general productivity work. They currently manage subscriptions personally and have never compared plans deeply.

### Direct Quotes
- “I use ChatGPT almost daily now.”
- “I never really track how much I spend monthly.”
- “The biggest issue is that these tools slowly become expensive.”

### Most Surprising Thing
The most surprising insight was that even frequent users often treat AI subscriptions like small invisible expenses rather than something actively optimized.

### What It Changed About My Design
This interview made me focus more on:
- monthly vs annual savings comparisons
- “healthy spending” messaging
- low-friction onboarding without login

It also reinforced the idea that many users may not want a complicated analytics platform and instead prefer instant recommendations.

## Interview 3 — Student Developer Using Cursor

**Stage:** Individual developer building coding projects

The third interview was with a friend who mainly uses Cursor for development workflows and AI-assisted coding. They personally pay for subscriptions and occasionally test different AI tools depending on coding needs.

### Direct Quotes
- “I switched from Cursor back to ChatGPT for some tasks.”
- “I don’t really know my total monthly AI spend.”
- “AI pricing keeps increasing and there are too many plans now.”

### Most Surprising Thing
The most interesting insight was how frequently users switch tools without properly comparing long-term pricing or workflow efficiency.

### What It Changed About My Design
This interview influenced:
- the benchmark comparison feature
- recommendation explanations
- support for multiple AI platforms

It also reinforced the need for a shareable audit page because users often discuss AI tools and pricing with friends or online communities before making decisions.
